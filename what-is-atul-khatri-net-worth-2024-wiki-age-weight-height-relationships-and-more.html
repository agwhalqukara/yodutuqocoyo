    FAQs about Atul Khatri

    Who is Atul Khatri?

    Atul Khatri is a prominent stand-up comedian from Mumbai, India. Known for his storytelling and observational humor, Khatri draws on his experiences as an engineer, MBA, and former CEO. He has performed at major comedy clubs and festivals worldwide, including the Hong Kong International Comedy Festival and the Melbourne International Comedy Festival.

    How did he start his comedy career?

    Khatri began his comedy career relatively late, taking up stand-up comedy as a new year’s resolution in 2012. His debut performance at an ‘open mic’ event was a success, and he has since performed in over 400 shows across 10 countries.

    What are some highlights of Atul Khatri’s career?

    He has been a part of various comedy shows and specials, such as EIC: Outrage and COMEDIANS of the world on Netflix, where his special The Happiest Ending was featured. Khatri was also the first Indian to perform at the 8th Annual Hong Kong International Comedy Festival.

    Has he won any awards?

    Yes, he was the winner of ‘CEO’s Got Talent’ Season 1 held by FremantleMedia in 2014 and was a finalist at the Melbourne International Comedy Festival held in Delhi in 2012.

    What controversies has he been involved in?

    In 2020, Khatri faced criticism for a tweet about Kangana Ranaut’s sister, Rangoli Chandel, which attracted significant backlash on social media platforms.

    Is Atul Khatri married?

    Yes, he is married to Shaguna Khatri, a hairstylist and sourdough baker. They have two daughters, Mishti and Diya.

    Does he have any hobbies or interests?

    Khatri is known to be a cycling enthusiast and an animal lover, with a pet dog named Butter Khatri. He is also involved in an initiative with his sister to spread mental health awareness among students.

    Where does he get his comedic material from?

    Khatri often draws material from current affairs, political jokes, and the diverse culture of India. He believes that there is a wealth of material available in the country’s news and everyday life.

    What platforms feature Atul Khatri’s work?

    Khatri’s stand-up comedy videos can be found on his YouTube channel, which boasts over 3 lakh subscribers. He has appeared in commercials for various brands and has been part of comedy reality shows like LOL – Hasse Toh Phasse and One Mic Stand on Amazon Prime Video.
