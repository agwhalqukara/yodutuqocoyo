    Gee Money is an American radio host, event host, and actor, best known as a radio host of the weekday radio program, The AM/PM Workload Show, which was broadcast on the FM radio station PJD5 (Laser 101 FM) in St. Maarten starting in 1999. He was also the host of Heineken Green Synergy DJ Competition Finals on the cable TV network that was broadcast across the Caribbean.

    Gee Money Facts

  • His high school classmate was Rich Eisen, an American journalist and broadcaster who has worked for FOX, NFL, CBS, and ESPN.
  • He has interviewed many well-known people from the showbusiness on his radio program, The AM/PM Workload Show. Some of those were Johnnie Cochran, Jimmy Cliff, The Black Eyed Peas, Ne-Yo, Sean Paul, Damian Marley, Shaggy, Fabolous, Bill Bellamy, Akon, Fat Joe, A.J. Calloway, and Wyclef Jean.
  • As an actor, Gee Money has appeared in movies such as Astoria Fix (1997), Mixing Nia (1998), and The Panman: Rhythm of the Palms (2007). The latter was the most successful, having won 2 awards in June 2008, “Best Feature Film” at the Hollywood Black Film Festival and “Best Score” at the Brooklyn International Film Festival.
  • In 2004 and 2007, Gee Money was awarded the Best of The Best Award for “Best Local Radio Host” in St. Maartens.
  • He is often confused with a Baton Rouge, Louisiana rapper, Garrett J. Burton, also known as Gee Money or Da Real Gee Money. Garrett was killed in a shooting on September 10, 2017, and shared the same last name with Gary, although it was never confirmed whether they were related. An associate of the rapper NBA YoungBoy was arrested for the murder.
  • He is a big fan of wine and is a part of an alcohol brand named Black Bottle Boys.
  • Gee Money was a big fan of Game of Thrones (2011-2019) and could hardly wait for the 8th season to premiere in April 2019.
  • Gee Money prefers Popeye’s Louisiana Kitchen over Chick-fil-A.
  • He and The Notorious B.I.G. were good friends and he mourns his passing every year on March 9.
  • He joined the Guardian Group Walk & Run event and finished a 3K mile race in June 2019, which was how he stays in shape.
