    Fred Couples is an accomplished American professional golfer born on October 3, 1959. He has won 64 professional tournaments, including the Masters Tournament in 1992 and the Players Championship in 1984 and 1996. Couples was nicknamed “Boom Boom” due to his long and accurate driving ability off the tee during his prime years.

    In 2011, Couples won his first senior major at the Senior Players Championship, and he followed it up in 2012 by winning the Senior Open Championship. He was inducted into the World Golf Hall of Fame in 2013, solidifying his legacy in the sport.

    Couples grew up in Seattle, Washington, and his signature loose and rhythmic swing was developed at the Jefferson Park golf course. He was self-taught and never hired a swing coach, relying on his natural talent and determination. 

    He attended O’Dea High School in Seattle before accepting a golf scholarship to the University of Houston, where he roomed with Blaine McCallister and Jim Nantz, who would become a future PGA Tour player and CBS television broadcaster, respectively.

    As a 19-year-old amateur, Couples beat PGA Tour veteran Don Bies in a playoff to win the 1978 Washington Open at the Glendale Country Club in Bellevue. This victory was a sign of things to come for the talented golfer, who would go on to have an illustrious career in the sport.
